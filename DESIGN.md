# Ernest Style Reference

This document describes the visual style of the Ernest app based on its source files only. It is a style reference, not an implementation plan.

## Overall Feel

Ernest feels calm, editorial, and intentionally crafted. It reads more like a thoughtful desktop workspace than a technical dashboard. The interface is soft and composed rather than glossy, loud, or overloaded.

Its personality comes from restraint:

- a pale sage outer canvas
- white card surfaces
- one dark forest-green accent
- one elegant serif wordmark moment
- compact rounded controls
- very little unnecessary variation

Ernest does not rely on spectacle. Its tone is quiet, clear, and slightly premium.

## Core Palette

Ernest uses a very small palette.

- Background canvas: `#E4ECE6`
- Primary surface / card: `#FFFFFF`
- Primary accent: `#1A4731`
- Foreground text: `#09090B`
- Secondary neutral: `#F0F0F0`
- Muted neutral: `#F4F4F5`
- Border / input line: `#E4E4E7`

The important part is not just the colors themselves, but their discipline.

- Green is scarce and intentional.
- White does most of the visual work.
- Light gray is used for quiet controls and utility states.
- The app does not spread the accent color everywhere.

## Emphasis Model

Ernest has a strict emphasis hierarchy.

- Strong green is for primary actions and key emphasis.
- White is for the main working surfaces.
- Neutral gray is for quieter controls, hover states, and utility areas.
- Text stays near-black by default.

This is what makes the interface feel controlled. The accent is not a theme wash; it is a deliberate signal.

## Layout Philosophy

Ernest is built as a row of independent rounded cards sitting on a padded outer canvas.

- The shell uses visible outer padding.
- Cards are separated by clear gaps.
- Panels feel like separate objects, not sections cut out of one large slab.

Structure comes from:

- spacing
- gaps between cards
- contrast between the canvas and white surfaces
- consistent rounded geometry

It does not depend on heavy dividers or dense chrome.

## Card System

The card system is one of the most important parts of Ernest's identity.

- Main panels are white, rounded, and softly contained.
- Corners are generous and continuous.
- Borders are subtle.
- Shadows are faint and supportive, not dramatic.

The cards feel soft and settled, like sheets or panels placed on a work surface.

## Shape Language

Ernest consistently uses rounded rectangles.

- Large cards use generous radii.
- Compact controls use smaller rounded corners.
- Buttons feel geometric and tidy.
- Nothing feels sharp, spiky, or overdesigned.

This consistency makes the interface feel cohesive and predictable.

## Typography

Ernest mixes utility typography with one editorial accent.

- Most UI text uses a clean system sans.
- The product wordmark uses Spectral Extra Light.

That serif wordmark is important. It gives Ernest a literary, authored quality that breaks away from generic SaaS or dashboard styling.

Typography is otherwise restrained:

- body text is compact and readable
- labels are clean and direct
- hierarchy comes from weight, size, and spacing rather than decorative styling

## Sidebar Style

The sidebar is a single white rounded card with a calm vertical rhythm.

Its top section is compact and deliberate:

- wordmark on the left
- collapse toggle on the right

Below that, the sidebar stacks controls with regular spacing.

- the first strong visual object is the primary action
- secondary controls are quieter
- the list area stays visually subordinate to the primary action

The sidebar does not feel like a noisy tool tray. It feels like a structured navigation column.

## Main Panel Style

The main panel is the dominant card in the composition.

- It has the same overall surface language as the sidebar.
- It feels roomier and more singular.
- It is calmer inside than many dashboard-style products.

Even when the app is functionally complex, the main panel tries to feel like one continuous work surface rather than a collage of mini-panels.

## Controls

Ernest's controls are compact, precise, and rounded.

- Icon buttons are square or nearly square.
- They use rounded corners rather than circles.
- Primary controls use the forest green fill with white content.
- Secondary controls use light neutral fills or white.

Buttons feel crisp, intentional, and proportional. They do not look like default system chrome, but they also do not feel decorative.

## Spacing Rhythm

Ernest is strongly spacing-led.

- side insets are consistent
- vertical gaps are modest and regular
- top rows are compact
- content breathes because the spacing system is steady

This is a big part of why the interface feels composed. Ernest does not need many dividers because spacing is doing the organizational work.

## Motion

Motion in Ernest is smooth, architectural, and controlled.

- panels resize with continuity
- labels fade rather than pop
- controls morph rather than abruptly swap

Animation is present, but not theatrical. It exists to preserve spatial understanding and make the UI feel alive without becoming noisy.

## Interaction Tone

Ernest's interaction tone is gentle and precise.

- hover states are subtle
- active states are legible but restrained
- the interface does not shout
- the app feels responsive without trying to impress through effects

This gives it a mature, confident feel.

## What Makes Ernest Distinctive

Ernest's style is not just "green plus rounded corners."

It is the combination of:

- pale sage canvas
- white card system
- disciplined forest-green emphasis
- one editorial serif brand voice
- compact rounded controls
- soft but consistent spacing
- minimal unnecessary variation

The result is an interface that feels calm, premium, and unusually thoughtful.

## Summary

Ernest is a restrained card-based desktop UI with editorial softness and precise operational clarity.

Its defining qualities are:

- calmness
- discipline
- low visual noise
- intentional emphasis
- clear spacing rhythm
- a quiet but distinctive brand voice

The most important takeaway is that Ernest succeeds through restraint. It uses very few visual ideas, but applies them consistently.
